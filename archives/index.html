About Me

I am a graduate student studying at the University of Pittsburgh, School of Computing and Information. My major is Infomation Science. I will graduate at December 2022, and am applying for Ph.D. programs. I received two Bachelor’s degrees of Computer Science and Computing and Information Science during my undergraduate period.

I mainly do research in Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Bayesian modeling fields. I think NLP is an important field that can make machines not only utilize quintillions bytes of data generated by human each day but also learn human’s past knowledge and experience, to solve the real-world problems. I have made a project of sentiment analysis of Tweets posted during Covid-19 pandemic, and a project of detecting unfair terms in the police union contracts. For Bayesian modeling, I learned a lot about it at my machine learning course, and I am currently working on a review of Bayesian optimization.

I am currently doing research in Dr. Yuru Lin’s PICSO lab at our school. I myself have finished a paper of using Transfer Learning to apply BERT models to downstream tasks, and it has been delivered to be reviewed.

Also, I am interested in most of the general directions in machine learning, such as Applications of ML in Medicine and Biology, Trustworthy ML, Deep Learning, and Reinforce Learning. I am still a “curious” student in the ocean of machine learning, so I am definitely willing to explore and learn those “exciting and magic” pieces of this ocean. Possible opportunities in all of these fields are appreciated.
